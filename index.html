3-Agent Decline-Resolution & Root-Cause Orchestrator
Solo-built & operated · daily production, 20-person team
Multi-Agent Orchestration
Problem
Wrongly-declined ("insulted") customers reach out across Jira / Slack / case-notes, and a team spent 20–30 minutes per case manually reconstructing which step of the risk pipeline fired and why — high volume, judgment-light.
Approach
A 3-agent pipeline built with Graph-RAG / Self-RAG, semantic routing, guardrails and self-correction (Claude + GPT): an intake agent routes by which risk step caused the insult and writes structured JSON to Airtable; an investigator agent walks a knowledge graph of every checkpoint model and queries the data lake with Splunk-log fallback to reconstruct the full decision trace; an analytics agent mines false-positive patterns and auto-generates QuickSight / HTML reports. Scheduled via Airflow.
Insight
The analytics agent surfaced a mid-value-biller insult gap; the targeting fix it proposed was adopted and A/B-validated.

02 — Case Study

Stopping an Account-Takeover Attack in 3 Weeks

A deep-dive into one high-stakes incident: turning a weekly-monitoring anomaly into a deployed, causally-measured mitigation. Framed in transferable terms — the same playbook applies to any rare-event / anomalous-behavior problem.

Account-Takeover (ATO) Attack Mitigation
Investigated, modeled, deployed & measured · end-to-end owner
Anomaly Detection · Production ML
Signal
Weekly loss-monitoring flagged a transaction-loss / return spike concentrated in dormant-but-tenured accounts — idle accounts with good tenure and a pre-dormancy device that, once reactivated, committed fraud on their first transfers. Because they looked "established," the legacy decision-tree policy missed them entirely.
Investigation
I built a PySpark pipeline aggregating merchant, transaction, bank, and device dimensions over the incident window (millions of records), and engineered new device-footprint features — the top signal by Gini importance being a spike in distinct IPs per account over a short window. Loss concentrated in a handful of banks.
Model
Trained an XGBoost / Random Forest model and shipped a tier-differentiated policy (strictness by customer segment to protect good users) with a grey-zone MFA band (borderline scores step-up instead of hard decline). Partnered with engineering to productionize via real-time DynamoDB scoring; measured impact with a Difference-in-Differences design against a control group.

03 — Rigor

How I Keep Agents Trustworthy

Shipping an agent is the easy half. The hard half is proving it stays correct as prompts, models, and data drift. Every system above is governed by an explicit evaluation harness.

Evaluation Framework

RAGAS + ground-truth validation

  • Faithfulness — does each agent's output stay grounded in the retrieved evidence, with no invented facts?
  • Answer & context relevance — RAGAS-scored to catch standard-drift before it skews every downstream judgment.
  • Ground-truth validation — agent decisions scored against real downstream outcomes (returns / chargebacks), not just human agreement.
  • Drift & anomaly monitoring — post-deploy tracking of model-score / segment drift, missing data, and vendor-feed incidents with auto-alerts.

Production Discipline

What turns a demo into something a team relies on

  • Human-in-the-loop routing — agents report on clear cases and escalate ambiguity instead of guessing; no auto-execution on bans.
  • Orchestration over monoliths — specialized agents with narrow jobs, composed with semantic routing & self-correction.
  • Compliance guardrails — hard rules force compliance-sensitive cases to a human regardless of model output.
  • Scheduled & retried — Airflow-orchestrated pipelines with real operational handling, not one-off scripts.

04 — Foundations

Data Science Under the Agents

The agent work sits on a foundation of applied ML, causal experimentation, and data engineering. A few representative results, kept brief.

Causal experimentation
Rebuilt the A/B control-group framework after finding two-gate randomization held out only 0.25% validly — a Schedule-ID "sticky" + stratified conditional holdout with CUPED, implemented in the production Drools engine. Coverage 0.25%→5–20%, insult 6–7%→5.4%.
Product / growth modeling
Risk owner for a Recurring Payments launch — defined an activation metric, trained an XGBoost/GBDT model cutting loss 1.2→0.3 bps, churn 3.6%→1.2%, reactivating 23% of dormant users into a $2.4M/month line.
Risk decision engine
Own the production Drools engines (DRL/KJAR) governing $40B/yr; replaced a non-deterministic multi-rule routing defect with a priority-based resolution across 4+ teams — ops tickets −17%, recurrence eliminated.
Data engineering at scale
Built a billion-record PySpark ETL on Databricks into a multi-dimensional portfolio-health dashboard adopted by a 20-person cross-functional team — manual reporting 5–6 hrs/wk → ~1 hr.
Efficiency analytics
A time-windowed case-aggregation algorithm (PySpark/Databricks) cut auto-approval exposure 15%→5%, saved 16K cases/yr (~$120K), and lifted reviewer accuracy 80%→87%.
Early GenAI text mining
Built a PySpark + LLM-API pipeline (distributed GPT / Claude calls) extracting structured fields from unstructured analyst notes — an early prototype of the production agents above.
